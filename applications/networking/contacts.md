## Outreach Templates

### Cold Outreach (LinkedIn)
```
Hi [Name],

I came across your profile while researching [Company] - your work on
[specific thing] caught my attention. I'm a Senior SWE with [X years]
experience in [relevant area], currently preparing for roles at AI labs.

I'd love to learn about your experience at [Company], especially
[specific question]. Would you have 15 minutes for a quick chat?

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Warm Introduction Request
```
Hi [Mutual Contact],

I hope you're doing well! I noticed you're connected to [Target Name]
at [Company]. I'm preparing to apply there and would really value an
introduction if you're comfortable.

I can share context about what I'm looking for and why I think I'd be
a good fit. Would that be okay?

Thanks so much,
[Your name]
```

### Post-Conversation Thank You
```
Hi [Name],

Thank you so much for taking the time to chat today. I really
appreciated your insights about [specific topic discussed].

[Mention something specific you'll act on from their advice]

If you're comfortable, I'd be grateful for a referral when I apply
next week. I'll make sure my application reflects our conversation.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Referral Request
```
Hi [Name],

I hope you're doing well! Following up on our conversation - I'm
planning to submit my application to [Company] this [day/week].

Would you be comfortable submitting a referral for me? I can send
you my resume and any context that would help.

I really appreciate your support!

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Informational Interview Questions

### Questions to Ask
1. What's your day-to-day like at [Company]?
2. What's the team culture like?
3. What does success look like in this role?
4. What's the interview process like?
5. What advice would you give someone preparing to interview?
6. What do you wish you knew before joining?
7. What's your favorite part about working there?
8. Is there anything I should know that isn't obvious from outside?

### Questions to Avoid
- Asking for a referral before building rapport
- Asking about compensation directly (research separately)
- Asking questions easily answered by research
- Making the conversation all about you
