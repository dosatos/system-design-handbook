## Negotiation Strategy

### Primary Leverage Points
1. **Competing offers**: Most powerful leverage
2. **Market data**: Levels.fyi, Blind data
3. **Experience**: Relevant skills and achievements
4. **Timing**: Other deadlines creating urgency

### Negotiation Order
1. Get all offers before negotiating
2. Negotiate lower-priority offers first (practice)
3. Negotiate top choice last (most leverage)

### Red Lines (Non-Negotiable)
- [ ] Total comp below $400K
- [ ] Level below Senior
- [ ] Vesting cliff > 1 year
- [ ] No health insurance

## Beyond Base Compensation

### Things to Negotiate
1. **Signing bonus**: Offset forfeited compensation
2. **Start date**: Delay for current bonus/vesting
3. **Level**: Higher level = higher long-term comp
4. **Equity refresh**: Earlier or larger refreshes
5. **Remote flexibility**: May offset lower comp
6. **Equipment budget**: Home office setup
7. **Learning budget**: Conferences, courses

### Don't Forget
- 401(k) match differences
- Health insurance costs
- PTO policies
- Parental leave
- Stock purchase plans

## Decision Framework

### If Offers Are Similar
Prioritize:
1. Team and growth opportunity
2. Technical challenge
3. Company trajectory
4. Work-life balance

### If Offers Differ Significantly
Consider:
1. Total 4-year compensation (not just Year 1)
2. Equity risk/reward profile
3. Career trajectory impact
4. Personal factors (location, culture)

### Calculating Trade-offs
- 1 great team > $50K/year
- Strong equity upside > guaranteed cash
- Path to Staff > immediate cash
- Remote flexibility > $30K/year (if valued)
