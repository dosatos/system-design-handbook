## Referral Strategy

### Ideal Referral
1. Current employee at target company
2. Has worked with you directly
3. Can speak to your technical abilities
4. Willing to advocate internally

### Referral Ask Template
```
Hi [Name],

Hope you're doing well! I'm preparing to apply for a Senior SWE role at
[Company] and would love your support.

I know you're busy, so I wanted to ask - would you be comfortable
referring me? I can provide my resume and any context that would help.

I'm particularly excited about [specific thing at company] and think my
experience with [relevant experience] would be a great fit.

No pressure at all if the timing doesn't work - I really appreciate you
considering it.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Referral Timeline
- Week 5: Initial outreach
- Week 6: Follow up, get confirmation
- Week 7+: Coordinate with application submissions

## Interview Coordination Strategy

### Goal
Get all companies to final round in same 2-week window for maximum leverage.

### Tactics
1. **Stagger Applications**: 1-2 weeks apart
2. **Communicate Timelines**: Be honest about other processes
3. **Request Alignment**: "I have onsites scheduled for [dates]"
4. **Create Urgency**: "I expect to have decisions by [date]"

### Timeline Communication
**When Scheduling Phone Screen**
"I'm flexible for the phone screen. I do want to mention that I'm also in process with [other companies] and expect to be interviewing over the next few weeks."

**When Scheduling Onsite**
"I'm targeting to have all my onsites in [specific week] if possible. I have [Company X] scheduled for [date]. Could we align timing?"

**When Offer Arrives Before Others**
"Thank you so much! I'm thrilled about this opportunity. I want to make a fully informed decision - I have final rounds with [Company] next week. Would it be possible to have until [date +1-2 weeks] to decide?"

## Contingency Plans

### If No Response (2+ weeks)
1. Follow up via email to recruiter
2. Follow up via referral contact
3. Reach out to team members on LinkedIn

### If Rejection
1. Request feedback (politely)
2. Ask about future opportunities
3. Maintain relationship for future
4. Apply again in 6-12 months if appropriate

### If Process Moves Slower Than Others
1. Communicate timeline pressure professionally
2. Ask if process can be expedited
3. Consider asking for earlier stages

### If Process Moves Faster Than Others
1. Ask other companies to expedite
2. Request deadline extension from fast company
3. Be prepared to make decision with incomplete information
