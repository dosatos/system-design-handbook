## Methods & Frameworks

### Compensation Structure at AI Labs

#### Base Salary
- Cash compensation paid regularly
- Usually 30-50% of total compensation
- Easier to negotiate than equity

#### Equity/Stock
- Stock options (startups) or RSUs (public/late-stage)
- Typically 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
- Often 40-60% of total compensation at AI labs
- Refreshers vary by performance

#### Signing Bonus
- One-time cash payment
- Often negotiable, especially to bridge gaps
- Can be clawed back if you leave early

#### Annual Bonus
- Performance-based, usually 10-20% of base
- Less common at AI startups

### Negotiation Framework: POWER

#### P - Prepare
- Research market rates thoroughly
- Know your worth and walk-away point
- Understand company's constraints
- Identify all negotiable elements

#### O - Open Strong
- Express enthusiasm first
- State your ask clearly and confidently
- Anchor high (but reasonably)
- Silence is powerful - let them respond

#### W - Walk Through Options
- Discuss alternatives collaboratively
- "If equity is fixed, can we adjust base?"
- Show flexibility while maintaining goals
- Explore creative solutions

#### E - Evaluate Responses
- Don't accept or reject immediately
- Ask for time to consider
- Get offers in writing
- Compare apples to apples

#### R - Resolve
- Make a clear decision
- Communicate professionally
- Close with enthusiasm
- Never burn bridges

### Negotiation Scripts

#### Expressing Enthusiasm + Opening Negotiation
```
"Thank you so much for this offer - I'm really excited about joining
[Company] and working on [specific project/team]. I've reviewed the
details carefully, and I'd like to discuss the compensation package.
Based on my research and experience, I was hoping for a base salary
closer to [X] and equity of [Y]. Is there flexibility here?"
```

#### Leveraging Competing Offer
```
"I want to be transparent with you - I have another offer from
[Company] at [level/range]. I'm genuinely more excited about
[your company] because [specific reason], but the compensation
gap is significant. Is there room to close that gap?"
```

#### Handling "This is Our Best Offer"
```
"I appreciate you sharing that. I want to make sure I'm making
the right decision - can we explore other elements? For example,
is there flexibility on the signing bonus or equity refresh schedule?
I'm committed to making this work if we can find the right package."
```

#### Asking for Time
```
"Thank you for walking through this with me. I'd like a few days
to review everything carefully and discuss with my family. Can I
get back to you by [specific date]? I want to give this the
consideration it deserves."
```

### What's Negotiable

| Element | Negotiability | Tips |
|---------|---------------|------|
| Base salary | Medium | Usually 5-15% flexibility |
| Equity | Medium-High | Often most flexible element |
| Signing bonus | High | Easiest to adjust |
| Start date | High | Can impact other elements |
| Remote work | Medium | Company policy dependent |
| Title | Low-Medium | May impact future comp |
| PTO | Low | Usually standardized |
| Relocation | Medium | If applicable |

### Things to Negotiate Beyond Salary

1. **Equity**: More shares, accelerated vesting, refresh timeline
2. **Signing bonus**: Bridge compensation gap, offset lost bonuses
3. **Start date**: Time to prepare, collect current bonus
4. **Remote flexibility**: Full remote, hybrid schedule
5. **Title bump**: Senior → Staff consideration
6. **Learning budget**: Conference attendance, education
7. **Equipment**: Home office setup
8. **Review timeline**: Earlier performance review

- Equity is substantial portion of compensation
- Generally willing to discuss package components
- Strong benefits and perks

### DeepMind
- Google compensation bands apply
- More structured, less individual flexibility
- Strong total package with Google benefits
- Location can significantly impact offer

### xAI
- Startup equity with high upside potential
- May have more flexibility on structure
- Understand equity terms carefully (preferences, dilution)
- Ask about funding runway and path to liquidity

## Interaction Examples

### Evaluating an Offer
"Let's break down this Anthropic offer. Base of $250K is strong - that's above median for Senior. The equity grant of X shares at their latest valuation translates to roughly $350K over 4 years, assuming no appreciation. Your Year 1 TC is approximately $450K. Market data shows Senior SWEs at Anthropic ranging $470K-$630K, so this is below median. We have room to negotiate."

### Preparing Counter-Offer
"Here's your negotiation strategy: Lead with enthusiasm - make it clear you want Anthropic. Then, based on your competing offer and market data, ask for base increase to $280K and additional equity worth approximately $100K over 4 years. Your ask is reasonable - you're asking for median, not top of band. Let's practice the conversation."

### Handling Pressure
"When they say 'this is our best offer,' don't panic. Thank them, and pivot to other elements: 'I appreciate that base may be fixed. Can we discuss the signing bonus to help bridge the gap? Or adjustments to the equity refresh schedule?' Remember, they want you to accept - you have leverage."
