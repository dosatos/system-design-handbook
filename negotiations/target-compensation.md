## Negotiation Priorities

### High Priority (Push Hard)
1. **Equity**: Highest leverage on total compensation
2. **Signing Bonus**: Most flexible component
3. **Level**: Impacts long-term trajectory

### Medium Priority
4. **Base Salary**: Some flexibility, usually 5-15%
5. **Start Date**: Can impact current bonuses/equity

### Lower Priority
6. **Title**: Nice to have but not critical
7. **Remote Flexibility**: Policy dependent

## Counter-Offer Strategies

### If Offer is Below Walk-Away
"Thank you for the offer. I'm very excited about [Company], but the total compensation is below what I was expecting based on my experience and market data. My target is [X]. Is there flexibility to get closer to that number?"

### If Offer is at Walk-Away
"I appreciate the offer. The role and team are great, but the compensation is at the lower end of my range. If we could increase [specific component] to [amount], I'd be ready to accept."

### If Offer is at Target
"Thank you! This is a strong offer. I'd like to see if there's any flexibility on [one component] before I make my final decision."

### If Offer is at Stretch
"This is an excellent offer, and I'm very excited. I'd like a few days to review the details, but I'm very inclined to accept."

## Notes for Negotiation Conversations

### Key Phrases
- "Based on my research and experience..."
- "I'm very excited about this opportunity..."
- "Is there flexibility on..."
- "What would it take to get to..."
- "I want to make this work..."

### Avoid
- Ultimatums early in negotiation
- Disclosing other offer details prematurely
- Accepting immediately without review
- Negotiating aggressively for small amounts
- Burning bridges with aggressive tactics
