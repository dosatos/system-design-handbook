# Multiple Offers Scripts

Scripts for managing and leveraging multiple offers.

---

## Having Multiple Offers: Strategy

### The Ideal Position
- Multiple offers create leverage
- Allows you to negotiate from strength
- Gives you real alternatives
- Reveals your market value

### Key Principles
1. **Be honest** - Don't fabricate offers
2. **Be professional** - Don't pit companies against each other aggressively
3. **Be timely** - Coordinate timelines actively
4. **Be decisive** - Don't drag out indefinitely

---

## Coordinating Timelines

### Asking Company A to Accelerate
```
"I want to be transparent with you - I've received an offer from
another company with a deadline of [date]. [Your company] is my
first choice, and I want to make sure I can properly consider
this opportunity.

Is there any way to accelerate the process on your end? I'd love
to have your offer before I need to make a decision."
```

### Asking Company B for Extension
```
"Thank you again for the offer. I'm very excited about [Company B].

I'm in final stages with [Company A / another company], and I want
to make a fully informed decision. Would it be possible to extend
my decision deadline to [date]?

I want to give [Company B] the full consideration it deserves."
```

### If Company Won't Extend
```
"I understand you need an answer by [date]. I want to be fully
committed when I accept, so let me do my best to get clarity
from my other process.

Can I confirm with you by [day before deadline] either way?"
```

## Disclosing Competing Offers

### When to Disclose
- When it's true (never fabricate)
- When it helps your negotiation
- When you need to coordinate timelines
- When asked directly

### When NOT to Disclose
- When it might come across as playing games
- When the companies might talk (small industry)
- When you're not prepared to follow through

### Standard Disclosure Script
```
"I want to be transparent - I'm also in process with [other
company/companies]. I have [an offer / final round coming up].

I'm telling you this because [your company] is my top choice, and
I want to make sure we're aligned on timeline and compensation."
```

### Mentioning Without Specifics
```
"I do have another offer I'm considering. I'd prefer not to share
the details, but I wanted you to know I'm actively weighing options.

That said, I'm genuinely most excited about [your company]
because [specific reason]."
```

## Leveraging Competing Offers

### Using Offer to Negotiate Higher
```
"I want to be transparent - I have an offer from [Company] at
[$X total compensation]. The team and mission at [your company]
are more aligned with what I'm looking for.

If we can get to a similar range, specifically [$Y], I would
accept your offer."
```

### If They Ask for Details
```
"I'd prefer to keep the specifics confidential out of respect for
both processes. What I can say is that it's a competitive offer
in the [$X range].

What I'm hoping is that we can get to a package that feels fair
based on the market and my experience."
```

### If They Can't Match
```
"I understand there may be constraints on matching exactly. I'm not
asking for a dollar-for-dollar match - I'm genuinely more excited
about [your company] for [reasons].

What can we do to close the gap? Even partial movement would help
me make this decision."
```

## Making the Decision

### Narrowing to Final Two

**Questions to Ask Yourself**:
- Which company excites me more?
- Where will I learn and grow faster?
- Which team felt like a better fit?
- Which role has more impact potential?
- If compensation were equal, which would I choose?

### Final Discussion with Top Choice
```
"I've been doing a lot of thinking, and [your company] is my first
choice. I have another strong offer that I need to respond to soon.

If we can finalize the package today, I'm ready to commit.
Specifically, if we can get to [$X], I'll accept and decline my
other offer."
```

## Declining Offers Professionally

### Declining After Negotiation
```
Subject: [Company] Offer - Decision

Hi [Recruiter Name],

Thank you so much for the offer and for working with me through
the process. After careful consideration, I've decided to accept
another opportunity that aligns more closely with my current goals.

This was a genuinely difficult decision. I was impressed by
[specific positive - team, mission, technology], and I hope our
paths cross again in the future.

Thank you again for your time and consideration.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

### Declining If You Didn't Negotiate
```
Subject: [Company] Offer - Decision

Hi [Recruiter Name],

Thank you for the offer to join [Company]. After much deliberation,
I've decided to pursue another opportunity.

I really enjoyed meeting the team and learning about [Company].
The work you're doing on [specific project] is impressive, and I
wish you continued success.

Thank you for the opportunity, and I hope we can stay connected.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

### Keeping the Door Open
```
"I want to thank you for this opportunity. While I've decided to
go another direction this time, I was genuinely impressed by
[Company] and the team.

I hope to stay in touch, and perhaps our paths will cross again
in the future. I'd love to remain connected."
```

## Managing the Final Days

### Day-by-Day Example

**Monday**:
- Receive Offer A, request time until Friday
- Offer B in final round

**Tuesday**:
- Final round with Company B
- Begin negotiation with Company A

**Wednesday**:
- Receive Offer B
- Company A responds to negotiation

**Thursday**:
- Counter-negotiate with both
- Make decision internally

**Friday**:
- Final negotiation call with top choice
- Accept offer
- Decline other offer

## Accepting the Final Offer

### Verbal Acceptance
```
"I'm thrilled to accept the offer to join [Company]. Thank you
for working with me on the package - I really appreciate it.

Please send over the final paperwork. I'm excited to start on
[date] and join the team."
```

### Written Acceptance
```
Subject: Accepting [Company] Offer

Hi [Recruiter Name],

I'm delighted to formally accept the offer to join [Company] as
[Title], with the following terms:

- Start Date: [Date]
- Base Salary: $[X]
- Equity: [X shares]
- Signing Bonus: $[X]

Please send over the official offer letter and any documents I
need to complete. I'm looking forward to joining the team!

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

## Key Principles for Multiple Offers

1. **Stay organized** - Track all deadlines and communications
2. **Communicate proactively** - Keep all parties informed
3. **Be honest** - Never fabricate competing offers
4. **Be respectful** - Everyone talks in this industry
5. **Make a decision** - Don't endlessly delay
6. **Commit fully** - Once you decide, be all in
