# Behavioral STAR Template

Use this template for developing and delivering behavioral interview stories.

---

## STAR Story Template

### Story Title: ___

**Best For Questions About**:
- [ ] Leadership
- [ ] Conflict resolution
- [ ] Challenge/Problem-solving
- [ ] Failure/Learning
- [ ] Teamwork
- [ ] Initiative
- [ ] Technical decision
- [ ] Other: ___

---

### S - Situation (10-15% of response)

**Context** (1-2 sentences):
_When and where did this happen? What was the background?_

**Example**:
"At [Company], I was the lead engineer on the [Project] team. We were facing a critical deadline for [Product Launch] with [Specific Challenge]."

**Your Situation**:


**Key Details to Include**:
- Company and team context
- Timeline/urgency if relevant
- Stakes involved

**Avoid**:
- Too much background
- Irrelevant details
- Starting too early in the story

---

### T - Task (10-15% of response)

**Your Specific Role** (1-2 sentences):
_What were YOU responsible for? What was at stake?_

**Example**:
"As the technical lead, I was responsible for designing and delivering the core API within 6 weeks. The launch depended on this component."

**Your Task**:


**Key Details to Include**:
- Your specific responsibility
- What success looked like
- Why it mattered

**Avoid**:
- Being vague about your role
- Describing team tasks, not YOUR tasks
- Downplaying your responsibility

---

### A - Action (60-70% of response)

**Your Specific Actions** (5-8 sentences):
_What did YOU do? Be specific about YOUR actions._

**Structure**:
1. First, I [specific action]...
2. Then, I [specific action]...
3. When [challenge arose], I [how you handled it]...
4. I also [additional action]...

**Example**:
"First, I broke down the project into components and identified the critical path. I set up daily standups to track progress and surface blockers early.

When we discovered the existing authentication system couldn't handle our scale requirements, I proposed a new architecture using [specific technology]. I presented the trade-offs to stakeholders and got buy-in within two days.

I then worked closely with the security team to ensure compliance while meeting our timeline. I personally implemented the most complex component, the token validation service, while coordinating with three other engineers on the remaining pieces."

**Your Actions**:


**Key Details to Include**:
- Specific steps you took
- How you made decisions
- How you influenced others
- How you handled obstacles
- Technical details where relevant

**Use Strong Action Verbs**:
Led, designed, implemented, negotiated, convinced, analyzed, discovered, proposed, coordinated, mentored, escalated, prioritized

**Avoid**:
- "We did X" (use "I did X")
- Being vague about your contribution
- Skipping over the hard parts
- Not explaining your reasoning

---

### R - Result (15-20% of response)

**Outcome** (2-3 sentences):
_What was the result? Quantify if possible._

**Example**:
"We launched on time and the API handled 10x our projected load on day one without issues. The project was recognized in the company all-hands, and the architecture I designed became the template for three other teams."

**Your Result**:


**Quantification Ideas**:
- Revenue/cost impact ($X saved)
- Time saved (reduced from X to Y)
- Scale achieved (handled X users/requests)
- Adoption (X teams now use it)
- Performance improvement (X% faster)

**Include Learning** (optional):
"Looking back, I would have [what you'd do differently]. I learned [key insight]."

**Key Details to Include**:
- Measurable outcomes
- Recognition received (if any)
- Lasting impact
- What you learned

**Avoid**:
- Vague outcomes ("it went well")
- No quantification when possible
- Not connecting to broader impact

---

## Story Quality Checklist

- [ ] Story is 2-3 minutes when told aloud
- [ ] Situation/Task are brief, Action is most detailed
- [ ] Uses "I" not "we" for your contributions
- [ ] Includes specific details and numbers
- [ ] Shows your thought process and decisions
- [ ] Has a clear positive outcome
- [ ] Addresses the question being asked
- [ ] Practiced and can be delivered smoothly

## Delivery Tips

### Voice and Pace
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Pause between sections
- Show enthusiasm for the story
- Make eye contact (virtual: look at camera)

### Time Management
- Situation: 15-20 seconds
- Task: 15-20 seconds
- Action: 90-120 seconds
- Result: 20-30 seconds
- **Total: 2-3 minutes**

### Handling Follow-ups
- Listen carefully to the question
- Give direct, concise answers
- It's okay to say "I don't remember the exact number, but..."
- Bridge to positive aspects

---

## Practice Method

1. Write out the full story
2. Practice telling it aloud (record yourself)
3. Time it (target 2-3 minutes)
4. Get feedback from others
5. Refine and practice again
6. Practice answering follow-up questions
