### Mock Interview Loops

**Schedule 2 full mock interview days:**

Each loop should simulate a real interview day:

```
Mock Day Structure (4-5 hours):

10:00 - 11:00  Coding Round 1
11:15 - 12:15  System Design
12:15 - 13:00  Lunch Break
13:00 - 14:00  Coding Round 2
14:15 - 15:00  Behavioral
```

**After Each Mock:**
1. Get detailed feedback
2. Document areas for improvement
3. Practice weak spots immediately
4. Don't let the same issues appear twice

### Focus Areas by Mock Result

| Feedback | Action |
|----------|--------|
| "Too slow on coding" | More timed practice, faster pattern recognition |
| "Unclear communication" | Practice thinking aloud, use structure |
| "Missed edge cases" | Create edge case checklist, slow down |
| "System design too shallow" | Study more deeply, practice trade-off discussions |
| "Behavioral stories weak" | Rewrite stories with more specifics |

## Week 11: Active Interviewing

### Interview Week Schedule

```
Day Before Interview:
├── Review company values and recent news
├── Light coding (2-3 easy problems)
├── Review your stories and questions
├── Get 8+ hours of sleep
└── Prepare materials (resume, notepad, water)

Interview Day Morning:
├── Light breakfast
├── 1-hour coding warm-up (medium problems)
├── Review company-specific materials
└── Arrive/login 10 min early
```

### During Interviews

**Coding Rounds:**
1. **Clarify** - Ask about input/output, edge cases, constraints
2. **Plan** - Explain approach before coding
3. **Code** - Write clean code, narrate as you go
4. **Test** - Walk through with examples
5. **Optimize** - Discuss time/space complexity, improvements

**System Design:**
1. **Requirements** - Clarify functional and non-functional
2. **Estimation** - Back-of-envelope calculations
3. **High-level design** - Draw architecture
4. **Deep dive** - Discuss 2-3 components in detail
5. **Trade-offs** - Explain decisions made

**Behavioral:**
1. **Answer the question asked** - Use STAR structure
2. **Be specific** - Names, numbers, outcomes
3. **Show growth** - What you learned
4. **Connect to role** - How this applies

### Interview Tips by Company

**OpenAI:**
- Discuss safety implications naturally
- Show practical engineering focus
- Reference recent OpenAI products/research
- Be ready for paper discussions

**Anthropic:**
- Weave in AI safety throughout
- Show depth over breadth
- Expect progressive difficulty
- Demonstrate intellectual curiosity

**DeepMind:**
- Strong ML fundamentals
- Research-oriented discussions
- Know your interviewers' work
- Show long-term thinking

**xAI:**
- Demonstrate velocity
- First-principles reasoning
- Show initiative in past work
- Handle pressure well

### Post-Interview Protocol

**Same Day:**
- Write down all questions asked
- Note what went well and poorly
- Send thank-you emails (optional but nice)

**Within 48 Hours:**
- Reflect on feedback received
- Practice any weak areas identified
- Follow up with recruiter if no timeline given

## Week 12: Final Interviews & Negotiation

### Pre-Final Round Preparation

**Research Interviewers:**
- LinkedIn profiles
- Published papers or blog posts
- Previous companies
- Prepare relevant questions for each

**Prepare Questions to Ask:**

| Stage | Good Questions |
|-------|----------------|
| **Technical** | "What's the hardest engineering challenge the team solved recently?" |
| **Team** | "How does the team decide what to work on?" |
| **Culture** | "How do you balance speed with quality/safety?" |
| **Growth** | "What does success look like in the first 6 months?" |
| **Future** | "What are the team's biggest priorities for the next year?" |

**Questions to Avoid:**
- "How many vacation days?" (ask HR later)
- "What does the company do?" (shows no research)
- "Is remote work possible?" (ask after offer)

### During Finals

**Senior/Leadership Interviews:**
- Show strategic thinking
- Discuss how you'd approach problems
- Demonstrate mentorship ability
- Ask about team challenges

**Cultural Fit Interviews:**
- Be authentic
- Show genuine excitement
- Connect your story to company mission
- Ask about what makes people stay

### Handling Multiple Offers

**Timeline Management:**
1. Ask for deadlines (typically 1-2 weeks)
2. Request extensions if needed (once, politely)
3. Inform other companies you have competing offers
4. Don't bluff about offers you don't have

### Negotiation

**Compensation Components:**

| Component | Negotiable? | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Base Salary | Yes | Usually 5-15% room |
| Equity | Yes | Often more flexible than base |
| Sign-on Bonus | Yes | Especially with competing offers |
| Start Date | Yes | Flexibility varies |
| Title | Sometimes | Harder at larger companies |
| PTO | Rarely | Usually policy-based |

**Negotiation Script:**

```
"Thank you for the offer. I'm very excited about the opportunity to work
on [specific project] with [specific person/team].

Based on my research and my [X years] of experience in [relevant area],
I was hoping to see the total compensation closer to [Y].

Is there flexibility here, particularly on [equity/base/sign-on]?"
```

**Key Principles:**
1. Always negotiate (politely)
2. Negotiate based on your value, not need
3. Use competing offers ethically
4. Get everything in writing
5. Don't accept on the spot - take 24-48 hours
