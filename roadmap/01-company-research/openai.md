## Company Culture

- **Talent Pool:** Global top 1% - ex-Olympiad winners, FAANG staff engineers, Stanford/CMU PhDs
- **Work Style:** Highly iterative, collaborative, fast-paced
- **Structure:** Decentralized, with variation by team
- **Remote:** Primarily in-person, some remote for infrastructure/policy roles

---

## Key Technical Requirements

### Must-Have Skills
- **Python** - Primary language
- **Own problems end-to-end** - Full ownership mentality
- **Move fast in ambiguity** - Loosely defined environments, competing priorities
- **ML research OR infrastructure OR performance optimization**

### Preferred Experience
- Large-scale ML systems
- Model training infrastructure
- API development and scaling
- Production systems at scale

## What OpenAI Values

> "We are looking for people who own problems end-to-end, and are willing to pick up whatever knowledge you're missing to get the job done."

> "Self-starters who care deeply about the end user experience and take pride in building products to solve customer needs."

## Interview Tips Specific to OpenAI

1. **Mission alignment is critical** - Be prepared to discuss why AGI safety matters to you
2. **Practical over theoretical** - Focus on real-world engineering, not obscure algorithms
3. **Read their blog** - Discuss recent research and products
4. **Safety mindset** - Show you think about implications of your work
5. **Take recruiter tips seriously** - They give specific guidance

---

## Sample Interview Questions

- "Design a system that allows employees to ask questions about internal company documents" (RAG system)
- "How would you approach distributed training for a model that doesn't fit on a single GPU?"
- "What are the risks of deploying LLMs at scale, and how would you mitigate them?"
