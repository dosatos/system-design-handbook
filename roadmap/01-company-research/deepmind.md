## Company Culture

- **Research-Centric:** Academic environment with long-term vision
- **Interdisciplinary:** Teams span ML, neuroscience, physics, biology
- **Publication-Focused:** 233+ research publications
- **Collaborative:** Frequent research discussions, cross-team work
- **Resources:** Massive compute and research partnerships

### What Sets DeepMind Apart
> "Research-Centric Visionary... investment in compute and research partnerships underscores a dedication to leadership in sustainable advances rather than immediate market dominance."

## Key Technical Requirements

### Research Engineer
- **Education:** BSc, MSc, or PhD in CS, stats, ML, or related fields
- **Skills:** Combination of engineering, mathematical, and research abilities
- **Role:** Bridge between theory and implementation

### Software Engineer
- **Languages:** Python, C++, some Java familiarity
- **Frameworks:** TensorFlow, PyTorch, NumPy, scikit-learn
- **Focus:** Reliable, efficient software built for scale

### Core Competencies
- Deep understanding of ML and deep learning
- Probability mastery (distributions, Bayes' theorem, Markov processes)
- Distributed computing experience
- Strong communication for interdisciplinary work

## Interview Tips Specific to DeepMind

1. **Research your interviewers** - Understand their work and ask relevant questions
2. **ML Fundamentals are tested** - Optimization, regularization, loss functions, transformers
3. **Prepare for ML Debugging** - "Most out of distribution" round, often "stupid" bugs
4. **Coding is LeetCode Medium** - Not as hard as other AI labs
5. **Show research thinking** - Discuss papers, hypotheses, experimental design
6. **Block prep time** - "I blocked a month... this had a huge impact on my results"

## Sample Interview Questions

### Coding
- String parsing: evaluate mathematical expressions
- Implement gradient descent for logistic regression

### ML Fundamentals
- Explain optimization algorithms (Adam, SGD, etc.)
- Discuss regularization techniques and when to use them
- Transformer architecture deep dive

### ML Debugging
- Given buggy training code, identify and fix issues
- Often "stupid" bugs (off-by-one, wrong dimension, etc.)

### Research Discussion
- Discuss a paper you've read recently
- How would you approach [open research problem]?
