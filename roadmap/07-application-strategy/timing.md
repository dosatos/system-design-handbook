## Optimal Timing

### Best Times to Apply

**Quarterly Patterns:**
- **Q1 (Jan-Mar):** Strong hiring, new budgets
- **Q2 (Apr-Jun):** Good hiring momentum
- **Q3 (Jul-Sep):** Strong hiring, pre-year-end push
- **Q4 (Oct-Dec):** Slower, especially December

**Best Months:** January, March, April, September, October

**Avoid:** December (holidays), August (vacations)

### Company Events to Watch

**Apply after:**
- Major funding rounds (increased hiring)
- Product launches (team expansion)
- Public company earnings (growth signals)
- Conference announcements (project visibility)

## Pre-Application Checklist

### Before You Apply (6-8 weeks of prep minimum)

**Technical Readiness:**
- [ ] Completed at least Phase 1-2 of prep (6 weeks)
- [ ] Solved 100+ coding problems
- [ ] Done 2+ system design practice sessions
- [ ] Completed 2+ mock interviews

**Materials Ready:**
- [ ] Resume tailored for AI companies
- [ ] GitHub profile polished
- [ ] 1-2 portfolio projects presentable
- [ ] LinkedIn updated

**Knowledge:**
- [ ] Researched target companies thoroughly
- [ ] Read recent engineering blogs
- [ ] Understand company culture and values
- [ ] Prepared behavioral stories

## Application Strategy

### Recommended Order

**Don't start with your top choice!**

```
Week 1-2: Practice companies
• Companies you'd take but not top choice
• Use to calibrate and get feedback
• Iterate on weak points

Week 3-4: Preferred companies
• Apply to top choices after initial interviews
• You'll be warmer and more prepared
• Better sense of what companies look for

Week 5-6: Align timing
• Try to schedule final rounds close together
• Gives you leverage in negotiation
• Avoid making decisions with time pressure
```

### How Many Applications?

**Recommended:** 5-8 companies total

| Too Few | Just Right | Too Many |
|---------|------------|----------|
| Limited options | 5-8 companies | 10+ |
| No negotiation leverage | Can compare offers | Can't prepare properly |
| High stakes on each | Manageable workload | Interview burnout |

### Parallel vs Sequential

**Parallel (Recommended):**
- Apply to multiple companies at once
- Schedule to align interview stages
- More leverage, faster resolution

**Sequential:**
- Only if you have a clear #1 choice
- Get practice at other companies first
- Risk: Timing misalignment

## Application Channels

### Ranked by Effectiveness

| Channel | Effectiveness | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| **Referral** | ★★★★★ | Best conversion rate |
| **Company website** | ★★★★ | Shows direct interest |
| **LinkedIn (with connection)** | ★★★ | If you know someone |
| **Recruiter outreach** | ★★★ | If they contact you |
| **Job boards** | ★★ | More competition |
| **LinkedIn Easy Apply** | ★ | Low signal |

### Maximizing Each Channel

**Referral:**
1. Ask referrer to submit your application
2. Provide them with your resume and bullet points
3. Apply through website as well (sometimes required)
4. Follow up with referrer after applying

**Direct Application:**
1. Apply through company careers page
2. Include cover letter if option exists
3. Follow up with connections after applying
4. Be patient but track progress

### Stages to Track

```
Applied → Recruiter Screen → Technical Screen →
Onsite → Offer → Negotiation → Decision
```

### Red Flags

- No response after 2 weeks (follow up)
- Process stalling (check in with recruiter)
- Vague feedback (ask for specifics)
- Rushed timeline (negotiate if needed)

## Following Up

### When to Follow Up

| Situation | Wait Time | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| After application | 1-2 weeks | Polite check-in |
| After interview | 24-48 hours | Thank you note |
| Waiting for next step | 1 week | Check-in with recruiter |
| After final round | 3-5 days | Ask for timeline |

### Follow-Up Templates

**After Application:**
```
Hi [Recruiter],

I applied for the [Role] position on [date] and wanted to follow up
on my application status. I'm very excited about the opportunity to
work on [specific project/team].

Please let me know if you need any additional information from me.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**After Interview:**
```
Hi [Interviewer name],

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today about the [Role]
position. I really enjoyed our discussion about [specific topic].

Our conversation reinforced my excitement about [specific aspect of role].
Please don't hesitate to reach out if you have any follow-up questions.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Handling Multiple Offers

### Ideal Scenario Setup

1. **Apply in waves** - Start with practice companies
2. **Communicate timelines** - Tell recruiters about other processes
3. **Align final rounds** - Try to schedule within same 1-2 weeks
4. **Honest communication** - Don't play games

### When You Have Multiple Offers

**Immediately:**
1. Thank all companies for the offers
2. Ask for decision deadlines
3. Request extensions if needed (politely)

**Decision Process:**
1. Compare total compensation (use levels.fyi)
2. Consider role, team, manager
3. Think about growth and learning
4. Factor in location, lifestyle
5. Trust your gut on culture fit

### Requesting an Extension

```
Hi [Recruiter],

Thank you again for the offer - I'm very excited about the opportunity.

I'm currently in final stages with another company and want to make a
thoughtful decision. Would it be possible to extend my decision deadline
to [date, usually 1-2 weeks]?

I want to give [Company] the full consideration it deserves.

Best,
[Your name]
```

```
Weeks 1-6: Core preparation
Weeks 7-8: Company-specific prep, start applications
Weeks 9-10: Active interviewing (practice companies)
Weeks 11-12: Final rounds (preferred companies)
Week 13+: Offers, negotiation, decision
```

## Final Tips

1. **Don't rush** - Better to be fully prepared
2. **Communicate openly** - Recruiters appreciate honesty
3. **Take notes** - Track everything
4. **Stay organized** - Use a tracker
5. **Practice patience** - Processes take time
6. **Keep preparing** - Don't stop practicing during interviews
