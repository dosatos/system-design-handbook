# Referral & Networking Strategy

## Why Referrals Matter

**Statistics:**
- 30%+ of hires come from referrals (SHRM)
- Referrals have 10x higher conversion rate than cold applications
- AI companies have small, tight-knit teams where referrals carry weight

**Reality:**
> "The AI engineering market in late 2025 rewards production skills over credentials, depth over breadth, and connections over cold applications."

---

## Referral Sources

### 1. Existing Network

**Warm Connections (Best):**
- Former colleagues now at AI companies
- Former managers who moved
- Classmates/alumni
- Conference connections

**Action Items:**
- [ ] List all contacts at target companies
- [ ] Check LinkedIn for 1st/2nd degree connections
- [ ] Reach out to former colleagues

### 2. Building New Connections (6-12 months before)

**Through Open Source:**
- Contribute to company projects (vLLM, LangChain, etc.)
- Your code becomes your introduction
- Build relationships with maintainers

**Through Content:**
- Write technical blog posts
- Engage with engineers on Twitter/X
- Comment thoughtfully on company blogs

**Through Events:**
- Attend AI/ML meetups
- Conference networking
- Company-hosted events

### 3. Cold Outreach (Last Resort)

**Less effective but possible:**
- Personalized messages showing genuine interest
- Offer value before asking
- Be specific about why you're reaching out

## Outreach Templates

### Template 1: Warm Connection

**Subject:** Quick question about [Company]

```
Hi [Name],

Hope you're doing well! I saw on LinkedIn that you're now at [Company] -
congrats on the move.

I've been following [Company]'s work on [specific project], and I'm
considering applying for [role]. Before I do, I'd love to get your
perspective on the team culture and what they're looking for.

Would you have 15-20 minutes for a quick call this week?

No pressure either way - I know you're busy!

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Template 2: Alumni/Shared Background

**Subject:** Fellow [University/Company] alum - quick question

```
Hi [Name],

I came across your profile while researching [Company] - fellow
[University] alum here ([Year]).

I'm particularly interested in [specific work they've done] that you
mentioned in [their blog post/talk/paper]. I'm preparing to apply for
[role] and would love to learn more about your experience there.

Would you be open to a brief chat? I'm happy to work around your schedule.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

### Template 3: Open Source Connection

**Subject:** Follow-up from [project] contribution

```
Hi [Name],

I'm [Your name] - I recently had [X PRs] merged in [project] that you
maintain. Thanks for the thoughtful code reviews!

I'm exploring opportunities at [Company] and noticed you work there.
I'd love to learn more about the engineering culture and what you're
working on.

Would you have time for a brief conversation? I'm also happy to
continue contributing to [project] regardless.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Template 4: Cold Outreach (Engineer)

**Subject:** Your [blog post/talk] on [topic]

```
Hi [Name],

I really enjoyed your [blog post/talk] on [topic] - especially
[specific detail that shows you actually read/watched it].

I'm a [role] at [company] working on [relevant work], and your
insights on [specific point] directly helped me [specific application].

I'm exploring opportunities at [Company] and would love to learn
more about your experience on the [team]. Would you have 15 minutes
for a brief chat?

Either way, thanks for sharing your work publicly!

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Networking Best Practices

### The 6-Month Plan

**Months 1-2: Build Presence**
- Start contributing to open source
- Begin writing technical content
- Join relevant communities (Discord, Slack)

**Months 3-4: Engage**
- Comment on engineering blogs
- Engage with engineers on Twitter/X
- Attend meetups/events

**Months 5-6: Connect**
- Reach out to specific people
- Have informational conversations
- Build genuine relationships

### What NOT to Do

| Don't | Instead |
|-------|---------|
| Ask for referral immediately | Build relationship first |
| Send generic messages | Personalize with specific details |
| Only reach out when job hunting | Maintain ongoing connections |
| Ask without offering value | Share your knowledge too |
| Be pushy or desperate | Be professional and patient |

### What TO Do

1. **Offer value first**
   - Share relevant articles/resources
   - Help with their projects
   - Make introductions for them

2. **Be genuinely interested**
   - Ask about their work, not just getting a job
   - Listen and engage meaningfully
   - Follow up on what they share

3. **Make it easy for them**
   - Be specific about what you're asking
   - Provide your resume if appropriate
   - Suggest specific times for calls

4. **Follow up and follow through**
   - Send thank-you messages
   - Keep them updated on your progress
   - Maintain the relationship long-term

## After Getting a Referral

### Prepare Your Referrer

**Provide them with:**
- Your updated resume
- Specific role you're applying to
- 2-3 bullet points about why you're a fit
- Timeline for when you're applying

**Example Message:**
```
Thanks so much for offering to refer me! Here's what would help:

Role: Senior Software Engineer, Infrastructure
Link: [job posting URL]

Why I'm a fit:
- 7 years building ML infrastructure at scale
- Led distributed training project for 100B params
- Deep experience with PyTorch and inference optimization

Resume attached. Let me know if you need anything else!
```

### After Application

- Thank them regardless of outcome
- Keep them updated on process
- Don't put pressure on them
- Maintain the relationship

## Building Long-Term Relationships

### The Right Mindset

> "Focus on genuine connection, not just asking for referrals. Offer help and value first. Follow up and maintain relationships."

### Stay in Touch

- Share relevant content occasionally
- Congratulate on achievements
- Check in every few months
- Offer help when you can

### Pay It Forward

- Refer others when you're on the inside
- Share your interview experiences
- Mentor those coming up behind you
- Build reputation as helpful
